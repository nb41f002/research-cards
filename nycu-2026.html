03 · The Real Gap

The Conceptual Materialization Dilemma

AI lowered one barrier.
It raised another.

SUNO AI removed the need for music theory. But without precise prompts, students cannot translate inner emotion into output — a gap that reproduces, rather than dismantles, existing inequality.

Three Research Questions

Q1 Can Human-AI collaboration empower diverse learners to transcend linguistic barriers?
Q2 Can creative agency be restored to non-specialist student composers?
Q3 Can technological equity be actualized in a public junior high context?

05 · The Framework

Three-Tiered Cognitive Scaffolding

Three tiers.
Three barriers dissolved.

I have an idea → Tier I
THEMATIC → Tier II
PROMPT SITE → Tier III
CUSTOM GPT → A finished song

Each tier addresses a specific cognitive barrier: ideation paralysis, English-typing demand, and structural lyric formatting.

06 · Tier I
I Thematic Framework

9 themes.
180 starting points.

Calibrated to adolescent emotional life.

Students choose a sub-topic as an immediate emotional anchor — eliminating ideation paralysis entirely. The framework spans family, friendship, identity, dreams, loss, hope, anxiety, and more.

9 × 20 matrix Emotional anchors Zero blank-page anxiety
07 · Tier II
II Prompt Scaffolding Website

No English typing.
Just dropdowns.

Visual selection over linguistic production.

A Google Sites platform with language selector (CN / EN / KO / JA), genre tags, phonetic rhyme-scheme picker, and vocal arrangement dropdown. One-click prompt copy auto-generates a complete structured prompt. Mobile-accessible. No English prompt typing required.

Multi-language Genre tags Phonetic rhyme Mobile-first
08 · Tier III
III Custom GPT · SUNO Lyrics Writer

Mandarin in.
Any language out.

Adds structure — never alters creative content.

Accepts Mandarin input; outputs fully structured lyrics with markers [Verse / Chorus / Bridge] in any target language. Automatic Mandarin translation + romanized phonetics appended for vocal performance.

The Custom GPT adds only structure — leaving every emotion, every metaphor, every word of student authorship intact.
Structured output Auto-translation Romanized phonetics 7+ languages

11 · Findings

Beyond the Numbers

Three things happened.

I
Sustained Collective Flow

Across all 18 classes, qualitative observation revealed sustained collective flow states during Weeks 3–6 — with behavioral disruptions virtually absent. The challenge-skill balance is engineerable.

II
Genuine Creative Ownership

Three students composed entirely self-authored lyrics, using the Custom GPT solely for structural formatting — direct evidence of creative agency cultivated by the scaffolding, not replaced by it.

III
Autonomous Continuation

Numerous students continued creating after the curriculum concluded — including several classes initiating graduation song projects, faculty-recognized as artistically meritorious. Intrinsic motivation, genuinely internalized.
